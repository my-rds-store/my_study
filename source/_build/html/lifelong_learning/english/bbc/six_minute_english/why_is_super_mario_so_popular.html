Why is Super Mario so popular?¶

  • bbc

  • ibbc

Rob

Hello. This is 6 Minute English from BBC Learning English. I’m Rob.

Georgina

And I’m Georgina.

Rob

Now, Georgina, you recently mentioned 1 in one 6 Minute English programme about NFTs and that you had a collection 2 of Pokemon cards when you were younger.

Georgina

Yes – I did, and I still can’t find them. Why did you bring that up?

Rob

Well, Pokemon started out 3 as a video game series that turned into anime 4 movies and trading 5 cards among other things – and in this programme we’re talking about a video game character 6 that is iconic 7 – a word which means widely 8 known and recognised 9 . That character’s name is Super Mario.

Georgina

Ahhh I spent many hours of my childhood playing with Nintendo’s Super Mario or his rival 10, Sega’s Sonic the Hedgehog 11 .

Rob

Now, these days, video games are everywhere, and people of all ages enjoy playing them. There’s also competitive 12 e-sports events where gamers compete 13 for what are often 14 considerable 15 amounts 16 of money.

Georgina

Yes, and there are also streamers that appear 17 on platforms like Twitch and YouTube who have become celebrities 18 in their own right 19 .

Rob

Talking about celebrities 18 , I have a question about the famous 20 character we’re talking about in this programme. Many people remember Super Mario Bros 21 . as being the first time we saw Mario, but he first appeared 17 in another game – which was it? Was it:

a) The Legend of Zelda - 《塞尔达传说》
b) Donkey Kong; or    - 《大金刚》
c) Pokemon -《宠物小精灵》
Georgina

I’m not sure about that – I can’t remember him being in Pokemon, so I’ll go for a) The Legend of Zelda.

Rob

OK, Georgina, we’ll find out if you’re right at the end of the programme. So, we established 22 at the start of the programme that these days the video games industry 23 is thriving 24 .

Georgina

True – but it wasn’t always that way. It’s hard to imagine 25 now, but in the 1980s the console market 26 was struggling 27 , particularly 28 in the US.

Rob

Keza MacDonalds, video games editor for the Guardian newspaper 29 , explains [#f30] what was happening in the early 1980s (20世纪80年代初期). Here she is on BBC World Service programme You and Yours, speaking with Peter White(彼得·怀特).

Keza MacDonald

Well, back then, especially in America, there had been a flood of 31 games that were just not very high quality. One of the games that’s often 14 cited 32 as a factor 33 in the collapse 34 was this game called ET on the Atari, which was so bad they ended up 35 burying 36 thousands of copies of it in the desert 37 , because nobody liked it.

And, so we’d had that, especially in America, this didn’t happen so much in Europe, but in America just been lots and lots of software. None of it was all that great. There hadn’t been anything really revolutionary 38 in some years, so the video game boom 39 was really falling off a cliff 40 and Nintendo is what rescued 41 in the US especially.

Georgina

Keza MacDonald used the term a flood of – meaning a large number in a short period of time – to describe the number of games that were coming out.

Rob

She used cited 32, which means referenced or noted, when talking about the game ET being a reference for a factor 33 in the collapse 34 of the console market 26 .

Georgina

And she said boom 39 – a sudden period of growth. So as ET was mentioned 1 as a factor 33 in the collapse 34 , many people say that Super Mario Bros 21 . was the reason that video games really took off 42 , especially in the US.

Rob

It’s interesting 43 to consider what might have been if his creator, Shigeru Miyamoto(宫本茂）, had never created that character 6 . The question is, why is that game so popular, what made it so fun to play?

Georgina

Here is Keza Macdonald speaking again with Peter White, on BBC World Service programme, You and Yours, explaining why Mario is just so popular and what makes the original game so satisfying 44 to play.

Keza MacDonald

It’s just such a joy 45 to play. It’s running and jumping, and it’s the joy of movement 46 . When, when you play, even the original Super Mario Bros 21 , you just feel this sense 47 of joy in your movement, and it’s one of the greatest 48 games ever made. And a lot of games from 35 years ago are basically 49 unplayable now. They might have been a step to something greater [#f50] , but Mario was one of those few that really holds up 51 today as it did then.

Rob

Keza Macdonald said that some games from 35 years ago are unplayable – so, not possible to play them.

Georgina

But she said that Mario holds up – a term used to say that something’s standards 52 or quality has not lessened 53 .

Rob

It certainly 54 does hold up – in fact, I played it the other day 55 and I had lots of fun with it – it reminded 56 me of my childhood, and it’s still as good now as it was then.

Georgina

Which reminds 56 me of your quiz 57 question, Rob.

Rob

Yes, in my quiz question I asked Georgina which game had the first appearance 58 of that famous 20 plumber 59 , Mario.

Georgina

I went for a) The Legend of Zelda.

Rob

Which is wrong, I’m afraid! Mario’s first appearance was in Donkey Kong, and his creator, Shigeru Miyamoto, never thought he would be that popular.

Georgina

Well, I guess we’ve all really learnt 60 something today. Let’s recap 61 the vocabulary 62 from today’s programme about Super Mario, starting with iconic 7 – famously associated 63 with something and instantly 64 recognisable 65 .

Rob

Then we had a flood of which means a large amount of something in a short space of time .

Georgina

Cited means reference as or noted.

Rob

Boom 39 relates to explosion 66 and means a short period of sudden growth.

Georgina

Unplayable describes something that can’t be played or a game that is very difficult to enjoy.

Rob

And finally, holds up means that the quality or standards of something hasn’t changed and still looks good or plays well.

Georgina

That’s all for this programme.

Rob

Bye for now!

Georgina

Bye!
